292  /*
293  Perform a subalgebra reduction of the entries of the one row matrix M.
294  C should be a GB computed in high enough degree to handle the elements of M,
295  of an ideal of the form y_i - m_i (m_i is the lead monomial of f_i).
296  F should be a ring map R --> R, sending y_i to f_i.
297  M should be a matrix over the ring R, usually only involving the variables in the f_i.
298  R should be a ring containing the variables of the f_i, and the variables y_i,
299  with a monomial order eliminating the first set of variables (which is 'numparts' parts of the
300  monomial ordering).
301  numparts: number of parts in the monomial order of the original ring.
302  The resulting matrix will have no monomials which are in the subalgebra
303  generated by the monomials m_i, and each entry of M and the corresponding entry of the
304  result differ by an element of the subalgebra generated by the f_i.
305  */
